


Account Types: Copilot in Outlook only supports work or school accounts, as well as Microsoft accounts using outlook.com, hotmail.com, live.com, and msn.com email addresses. If you’re using a third-party email provider (such as Gmail, Yahoo, or iCloud), you can still use Outlook, but you won’t have access to the Copilot features in Outlook.
Thread Length: In the case of a very long email thread, the Summarize feature may not include older messages in the conversation. So, if you’re dealing with extensive email chains, be aware that Copilot might not summarize all parts of the thread.
Customization Options: Copilot in Outlook does not currently allow users to customize its settings like choosing between Creative, Balanced, or Precise modes. You’ll have to work with the default settings provided by Copilot.

Language Support: Copilot in classic Outlook primarily works with English language content. While it can handle other languages to some extent, its performance may not be as accurate or reliable. If you’re dealing with emails in languages other than English, keep this in mind.
Privacy Considerations: Copilot processes the content of your emails to provide assistance. While Microsoft takes privacy seriously, some users may have concerns about their data being analyzed by an AI. If privacy is a significant concern for you, you might want to limit Copilot’s access to sensitive information.
Contextual Understanding: Copilot’s understanding of context is based on the content within the email thread. However, it may not always grasp the broader context of your work or personal situation. Be prepared to provide additional context if needed.

Attachments and Embedded Content: Copilot primarily focuses on the text content of emails. It may not handle attachments (such as images, PDFs, or other files) or embedded content (like videos or interactive elements) within emails. If an email heavily relies on non-textual components, Copilot’s assistance may be limited.
Complex Formatting: Copilot aims to provide plain text responses. If an email contains intricate formatting (such as tables, complex styling, or HTML), Copilot might not fully capture the visual presentation. You may need to manually review and adjust formatting as needed.
